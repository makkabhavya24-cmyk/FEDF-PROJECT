Abstract
Understanding credit scores and the variables that influence them remains a challenge for many consumers due to complex formulas and a lack of transparent, interactive tools. This fragmented and obscure nature of credit reports often leads to confusion, incorrect assumptions, and poor financial planning. To address this, our project focuses on the Design and Development of a Web-Based Credit Score Calculator and Financial Health Analyzer. Our core aim was to successfully implement a unified, single-window website that dramatically simplifies the process of estimating, understanding, and improving credit scores for everyday consumers.
The platform is structured around four primary, fully interconnected financial inputs. It offers real-time scoring simulation for Monthly Income, Repayment History (total EMIs versus late payments), Credit Card Utilization (total limit versus credit spent), and Credit Mix (the number of active loans). The backend executes a deterministic credit scoring algorithm mapping values between 300 and 900. Crucially, the platform includes a dynamic suggestions engine that renders custom reasons indicating which factors are actively lowering the user's score.
The system is built using robust, modern web technologies including React.js for modular state-driven components, HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript (ES6+). To ensure security and convenience, the user's session profile is stored locally using the browser's native LocalStorage API, eliminating the need for expensive server-side databases or hard credit bureau queries that lower consumer scores. In summary, this project delivers a complete, reliable, and user-friendly digital utility that significantly enhances credit literacy and financial health.

CHAPTER - 1 INTRODUCTION
1.1 Background of the Project
Personal finance management has become one of the most critical aspects of modern household economics. In our current credit-driven society, an individual's financial health is summarized by a single credit rating, commonly known as a CIBIL score in the Indian market. This score, which ranges between 300 and 900, evaluates how reliably a consumer handles debt obligations. Financial institutions, including commercial banks, housing societies, and credit card issuers, utilize this score as a primary gateway to gauge risk.
Despite its absolute importance, the mechanisms behind credit score generation remain poorly understood by the average consumer. Most traditional bureau platforms require hard pulls on credit histories, which in turn lower the customer's score, or charge steep premium subscription fees. This creates an environment of exclusion where users cannot safely simulate different debt scenarios. Developing a client-side, interactive credit health analyzer allows individuals to dynamically adjust financial parameters and view their estimated credit rating without any hard pull registry or financial exposure.
1.2 Problem Statement
In today's digital personal finance market, consumers are forced to navigate multiple portals to verify their creditworthiness. These standard services suffer from major deficiencies:
  • Privacy Risks: Services demand highly sensitive identifiers (e.g., PAN cards, social logins) and monetize user profiles by selling them to credit brokers.
  • Absence of Scenario Simulation: Consumers cannot model how paying off a certain percentage of a credit card bill or a loan will impact their rating.
  • Cryptic Output Formats: Reports are presented with complex financial jargon, without explaining the specific root causes lowering the scores.
